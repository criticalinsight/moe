Serendipity

October 4, 2021 by Moe

a freckled lilac sky lies awake above your head, tonight. what are stars are now flaming, white spots growing larger moment by moment, flickering in the ocean-filled eyes that stare up at them. the dandelions in the evening wind always mocked you behind your back because you could only reach so far, like a leaf bound to the branch of a tree, only free from it when October closes down on the burning heat of summer, but the passing of the comets as their thin streaks of light left traces of ethereal beauty in the velvety-blue sky melted away their scornful gazes. there’s a secret underneath those otherworldly folds. a rhythm to the beam of the moon, the shimmering of the stars and soul-stirring gleam of the northern lights. a symphony to the dance of this mass of green I lay back on, the soft whispers of the breeze and howling of the gales. the quivering of the leaves, the ebb and flow of the ocean tides. “maybe the sea carries our dreams, and we all wait for the salt to soak into our ankles, when instead, we should be out there dancing with the waves”

Moe
