then comes the earth: the dark brown matter on which man traverses over, from which life is birthed and to which it returns. empty but brimming with life, quiet and varying; a bellow of shattered hopes, a sigh of a content soul….you are proof that the most hollow and breathless possess the most life, the most light—they are merely just hidden. you are more than degraded, weathered rocks, but a thousand splendid ends, and a thousand splendid beginnings
