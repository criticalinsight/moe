Overload

Sun

October 4, 2021 by Moe

golden globe, I weep for you. The salt inhabited pools that deprive of me clear sight are dedicated to you, as I spend countless silent nights staring at the golden rays you bestow upon that mere rock your flames ablaze for. I will not deny it, I am at fault. I preferred to have my being glistening and dripping wet in crystal light and found your lemon beams a curse, bearing an acidity that ate at my flesh until all that was left was soulless bone. I begged for the sky to be darkened and showered with those white spots of magnificence, and in the center of that breathtaking infinity, the celestial you endlessly boil harder for. mother of stars, heed my call. let my sorrows echo throughout the ever growing realm you dwell in and awaken your kin, for they too need to hear this unraveling of mine. they too need to realize that they worship who must not be totally worshipped, that they have their backs turned on she who brings forth all this beauty; it is your own embodiment that gives life to the arch of beauty that brightens up a mighty deluge, signifying the island of bliss and contentment that erupts only after a shipwreck. But it has taken a near eternity to have myself dawned in the already known truth. you are the spark that lightens up our blackened souls and perishes away the darkness, and from that burning light grew the flowers, even if not many, that encircle our concealed edges. yet you are loved by few. and yet your adoration for we underlings does not flicker.

—Sun, with these words I give you my all.

Moe
