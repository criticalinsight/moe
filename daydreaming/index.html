Daydreaming

October 4, 2021 by Moe

there are some moments, usually on the most inactive and weary of days, when my mind wanders back to the peaceful eighteenth century; I stand beside a weeping willow on a golden-lit Wednesday afternoon, watching intently at a shape in the distance, planted on bare, breathing brown earth. his eyes: a midday starry sky, gleaming with intense admiration as they pick on every bend, curve, curl and imperfection on the supernova bursting around him. how could anything be so beautiful, so heart-hammering? he questions himself, the running man whose print sonnets and lullabies to him. he reaches for his brush, his stained palette, and the small fabric canvas hidden within the red poppies he sits in. who would have thought a simple tool—a stick with hair strands—a non-living object—could breathe out such life, such light? —daydreaming about Claude Monet
