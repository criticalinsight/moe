Simplicity

October 4, 2021 by Moe

oh, the transience of life and happiness tugs at my core and discomfort clouds me; I long for the little things, simplicity. everyday I look over that vast field of dancing grass beyond these royal walls I’ve been imprisoned in. I dream of laying naked in them, with a smile —a real smile— painting my face, painting my skin in the sun’s sweet, warm gold. then those woods, another little kingdom I would be more than happy to be lost in for eternity, because I’ve never travelled in a place brimming with so much life. they say there’s a secret garden there, with a pond and a swing; my eyes water at the absence of my fingerprints significantly staining them all, proof of my presence. I wish to bury my toes in the dirty black soil, trace my fingers over the bark of the trees, sigh at the sound of the birds tweeting in the leaves…I can only imagine how much warmth and peace awaits me. and I can stay here no longer. I must leave here at once, I must! or I believe I shall perish soon. I am afraid of dying out of deep sadness and boredom, and, when I return to the soil, discover that I have not lived.

Moe
